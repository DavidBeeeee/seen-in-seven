# 7 Videos 7 Days $7 — Comprehensive Funnel Audit
### Through the lens of Russell Brunson's frameworks (Expert Secrets, DotCom Secrets, Traffic Secrets)

**Audited by:** Claude (channeling inner Brunson)
**Date:** March 2026
**Status:** Every page read line by line. This is the real talk.

---

## THE VERDICT UP FRONT

This funnel has a genuinely brilliant core mechanism (the MVO wizard that gives people a personalized script before asking for money) and the copy across Pages 1 through 6 is honest, warm, and well-written. The voice is authentic. The $7 price point is smart. The story sections are strong.

But there are structural gaps that a Brunson-trained eye catches immediately. These aren't copy tweaks. These are architecture-level issues that directly affect conversion rate, average cart value, and lifetime customer value.

Here are the 14 things I'd fix, in priority order.

---

## 1. THE TRUST BREACH: "No Upsell" vs. Actual Upsell

**Severity: Critical**
**Location: Page 1 Section 8 ("No Catch") vs. Page 4 (Build With Bee OTO)**

Page 1 literally says:

> "No upsell waiting on the next page."

Then Page 4 is... an upsell. A $250/month upsell.

This is the kind of thing that kills trust instantly and generates refund requests. Brunson is aggressive with OTOs but he never *promises there won't be one and then hits you with one anyway.* That's not persuasion, that's bait and switch, and the people most likely to notice are your best prospects: the thoughtful, detail-oriented ones who actually read your sales page carefully.

**The fix:** Remove the "No upsell waiting on the next page" line from Section 8. Replace with something that's still reassuring but doesn't make a promise you're about to break. Something like: "No hidden fees. No surprise subscriptions. You know exactly what you're getting before you pay." That's still powerful reassurance without the specific claim that contradicts your funnel architecture.

Also remove "No upsell" from the offer total sub-line on Page 1 (currently says "One time · No subscription · No upsell"). Change to "One time · No subscription · No recurring fees."

---

## 2. THE MVO PAGE HAS ZERO SOCIAL PROOF

**Severity: Critical**
**Location: Page 2 (the wizard)**

Someone comes from TikTok, goes through the wizard, gets their script, sees the "I'M READY FOR VIDEO 2" button... and the entire decision to click or not is happening with zero evidence that anyone else has ever done this.

No testimonial. No "4,217 scripts generated" counter. No "Join 300+ creators who started this week." Nothing.

Brunson would lose his mind. The moment of highest intent (they just built their script, they're emotionally invested, they can SEE themselves doing this) has the least social proof of any page in the funnel.

**The fix:** Add a single, tight social proof element between the CTA button and the video tracker. Not a full testimonial block. Something compact like:

> "Join 300+ creators who turned their Video 1 script into all seven."

Or a mini-testimonial: one line, one name, directly below the CTA note. This is the highest-leverage single addition you could make to the entire funnel.

---

## 3. MISSING: THE "IF ALL THIS DID WAS..." REFRAME

**Severity: High**
**Location: Should be on Page 3 (above form), just before the offer total**

This is one of Brunson's most powerful closing techniques and it's completely absent. The reframe works like this:

> "If all this challenge did was help you finally post your first video and prove to yourself that you can actually do this... would it be worth $7?"
>
> "If all it did was give you a system so you never have to stare at a blank screen wondering what to say... would it be worth $7?"
>
> "If all it did was put you in a room full of people doing the exact same thing so you never have to figure this out alone... would it be worth $7?"

Each "if all this did was" line isolates a single benefit and anchors it against the $7 price, making the full stack feel like a completely absurd deal. It works because it gives the reader permission to buy for just ONE reason instead of needing to justify the entire package.

**The fix:** Add an "If all this did was..." section on Page 3, between the future pacing block and the offer stack. Three lines maximum.

---

## 4. THE $7 TO $250 JUMP IS TOO STEEP FOR AN OTO

**Severity: High**
**Location: Page 4 (Build With Bee upsell)**

The standard OTO formula is 2x to 5x the initial purchase. You went from $7 to $250/month, which is 35x. In Brunson's world, that's not an OTO. That's a completely different funnel.

The reason this matters: the psychology of a one-time-offer works because the person just said yes to spending money, so the resistance to spending *a little more* is low. But $250/month doesn't feel like "a little more than $7." It feels like an entirely different decision, requiring entirely different justification, and it breaks the momentum of the "yes" they just gave you.

**The fix (two options):**

**Option A: Add a mid-tier OTO.** Create a $37 or $47 one-time product that sits between the $7 challenge and the $250/month coaching. This could be something like "The Complete Video Confidence Toolkit" or "The 30-Day Content Calendar Extension" — something that enhances the challenge without requiring ongoing commitment. Page 4 becomes the $37 OTO, and Build With Bee gets introduced later via email or after they complete the challenge.

**Option B: Reframe the $250 as a "trial."** Instead of presenting it as $250/month ongoing, present it as a single trial month. "Try one month of Build With Bee for $250. No contract. No commitment beyond 30 days. If it's not for you, you cancel and we part as friends." This lowers the perceived commitment even though the price is the same.

Option A is the Brunson play. Option B is the minimum viable fix.

## 5. THE ORDER BUMP EXISTS IN CONCEPT BUT NOT IN COPY

**Severity: High**
**Location: Page 3 (the bump seed mentions it, but there's no actual bump copy)**

On Page 3 there's a smart little paragraph that seeds the order bump: "the biggest reason people don't finish challenges like this has nothing to do with motivation or time. There are three specific patterns that kill follow-through, and we've put together something that addresses all three directly. You'll see it at checkout."

But where's the actual bump copy? Systeme.io has a native order bump feature, and you've got a comment in the HTML saying "Configure order bump in Systeme: 3 Reasons We Fail at $37" — but there's no copy written for that bump. The seed is planted and then never harvested.

**The fix:** Write the actual order bump copy. This should be 3–4 sentences that appear on the Systeme order form itself. Something like:

**YES, add "The 3 Patterns That Kill Your Challenge" ($37)**

"Most people who start a 7-day video challenge quit between Videos 2 and 4. Not because they're lazy, but because one of three specific patterns kicks in and they don't see it coming. This training identifies all three, shows you what each one looks like in real time, and gives you the exact response for each one so you can push through the moment everyone else folds. Add it now for $37 — it won't be offered again after checkout."

That last line is important. It creates genuine urgency because order bumps really aren't offered again.

## 6. NO URGENCY OR SCARCITY ANYWHERE IN THE FUNNEL

**Severity: High**
**Location: Entire funnel**

There is not a single urgency or scarcity element in this entire funnel. No countdown. No limited spots. No "this price is only available today." No cohort deadline. Nothing.

Brunson would say this is the single biggest conversion gap. People don't buy because they *want* to buy. They buy because they want to buy *and* they believe they need to do it now. Without urgency, "I'll come back to this later" wins every time, and "later" means "never."

Now, I know your brand is built on honesty. You don't want fake countdown timers or manufactured scarcity. But there IS legitimate urgency here that you're not using:

**The fix (honest urgency, three approaches you can combine):**

**A. Cohort urgency.** Run the challenge in cohorts. "The next cohort starts Monday. Join before then and you're in the group with everyone starting at the same time." This is real, it's honest, and it creates a genuine deadline.

**B. Momentum urgency.** "You just built your Video 1 script. It's sitting right there, ready to film. The longer you wait between seeing your script and actually filming it, the less likely you are to ever do it. This is the moment. Not tomorrow. Not this weekend. Right now, while the words are still fresh." This is psychological urgency based on a true behavioral insight.

**C. Price anchoring with time.** "This introductory price of $7 is available while we're building the community. Once we hit [X] members, the price goes to $27." Only do this if you actually intend to raise the price.

I'd use A and B together. They're both honest and they complement each other.

## 7. THE PERSONALIZED SCRIPT ISN'T ACTUALLY PERSONALIZED

**Severity: Medium-High**
**Location: Page 2 (MVO wizard result)**

The wizard asks four questions: Have you posted before? What's your blocker? Are you building a business? What's your name?

Then it generates the exact same script for everyone, with only their name swapped in. A first-time poster who's terrified of the camera gets the same script as a business owner who used to post consistently. The "personalized" label overpromises.

This matters because the wizard is your core mechanism, your "secret sauce," and the thing your entire funnel is built around. If someone goes through it and gets a generic script, the gap between expectation and delivery erodes trust right at the moment you need it most.

**The fix:** You don't need to build a full AI script generator. You just need 3–4 script variations based on their answers:

- **Never posted + scared of camera:** Script emphasizes vulnerability and "this is me starting even though I'm scared"
- **Never posted + don't know what to say:** Script emphasizes "I have things to say but never knew how to start"
- **Posted before + fell off:** Script emphasizes "I've been here before but this time I'm doing it differently"
- **Business owner + just needs to start:** Script emphasizes authority and "I've been helping people behind the scenes but never showed up publicly"

Four variations with the same structure. The name still gets swapped in. But now when they read their script, it actually reflects what they told you, and the "personalized" badge feels earned.

## 8. NO VISUAL PROOF OF THE WIZARD IN ACTION

**Severity: Medium**
**Location: Page 1 (Sections 5 and 7) and Page 3**

You describe the wizard extensively on Page 1 — three steps, how it works, what it generates. But you never SHOW it. There's no screenshot, no GIF, no video of someone going through it.

Brunson calls this "showing the thing." People need to see the product before they buy it, even (especially) at $7. The wizard is visually impressive. It has the dark UI, the progress dots, the loading animation, the script reveal. That's GOOD visual design. Use it.

**The fix:** Add a screenshot or short GIF/video of the wizard in action on Page 1 Section 5 (where you describe the three steps). Even a static image of the script reveal screen with a blurred-out name would be powerful. On Page 3, consider adding a smaller version above the offer stack — a visual reminder of what they just experienced and what they're about to unlock the full version of.

In Systeme, you can upload images directly into code blocks or use native image elements between blocks.

## 9. THE SOAP OPERA SEQUENCE DOESN'T ASCEND

**Severity: Medium**
**Location: soap-opera-sequence.html**

The 5-email sequence is well-written and follows the Brunson framework nicely: backstory, epiphany, hidden benefits, urgency, clean close. But it ends at Email 5 and goes silent.

What happens after Day 5? Nothing. The buyer is either mid-challenge or finished, and there's no email acknowledging either state. There's no "you just posted Video 3, here's what to expect next" email. There's no "you finished all 7, here's what people do next" email. And most importantly, there's no introduction of Build With Bee through the email channel.

**The fix:** Add two more email phases:

**Phase 2: Challenge Support (Days 3–7).** Two or three emails timed to when they're likely hitting Videos 3–5 (the danger zone where people quit). These should normalize the struggle and re-motivate.

**Phase 3: Ascension (Days 8–14).** After they've had time to complete the challenge, introduce Build With Bee as the natural next step. Not hard-sell. Story-driven. "Here's what happened to [name] after she finished the 7 videos and wanted to keep the momentum going." This is where the $250/month coaching gets its real introduction, in a context where the reader has already gotten results and can imagine wanting more.

## 10. PAGE 3 BELOW-FORM SAYS "NO AUTOMATIC RENEWALS" BUT DOESN'T SAY GUARANTEE

**Severity: Medium**
**Location: Page 3 Block 2**

The below-form copy says "one-time $7 payment with no subscription and no automatic renewals" and mentions "reach out to contact@DavidBee.me and it'll be taken care of." That's a guarantee buried inside casual language.

Brunson always makes the guarantee a SECTION, not a sentence. Even at $7, an explicit guarantee reduces friction.

**The fix:** Don't create a formal "30-day money-back guarantee" box (that feels corporate for a $7 product). But do make the guarantee its own visual beat. Something like a small callout card:

> **Not what you needed?** Email contact@DavidBee.me and I'll refund you personally. No form. No questions. No weird process. Just an email from me saying "done."

This takes the buried reassurance and gives it visual weight without feeling like a corporate guarantee policy.

## 11. THE DOWNSELL (PAGE 5) HAS NO RE-ENTRY PATH

**Severity: Medium**
**Location: Page 5 (Eventbrite mastermind)**

Someone declines the $7 offer, lands on the downsell page, and gets offered a free Eventbrite mastermind ticket. That's a thoughtful offer. But once they grab that ticket and leave, they're gone. There's no email capture. There's no way to bring them back to the $7 offer later.

**The fix:** The Eventbrite registration captures their email. Make sure that email feeds into a Systeme.io automation that sends them a "Hey, great meeting you at the mastermind" follow-up sequence that naturally re-introduces the $7 challenge. This isn't a funnel page fix — it's a backend automation fix. But without it, the downsell is a dead end.

Also: consider adding one line on Page 5 above the Eventbrite embed: "And if you change your mind about the challenge, the $7 offer is always at [URL]." Give them a way back.

## 12. ALL TESTIMONIALS ARE PLACEHOLDERS

**Severity: Medium (but becomes Critical at launch)**
**Location: Pages 1, 3, and 4**

Every testimonial in the funnel is marked [PLACEHOLDER]. This is obviously expected at this stage of development. But I want to flag the priority order for replacing them, because not all testimonials are created equal.

**Priority 1: Page 3 above the offer stack (Jennifer R.).** This is the single most important testimonial in the funnel because it sits at the decision point. Replace this first, ideally with a video testimonial that can be embedded.

**Priority 2: Page 3 below the stack (Sarah K. and Marcus T.).** These validate the result. Real names, real professions, real outcomes.

**Priority 3: Page 1 Section 7.** These validate the offer but are less critical because Page 1 is a free opt-in — the stakes are lower.

**Priority 4: Page 4 coaching testimonials.** These only matter for the upsell conversion, which is a smaller percentage of traffic.

**The longer play:** Once you have real people finishing the challenge, ask them to film a 30-second video testimonial using the wizard. Embed those on Page 1 and Page 3. Video testimonials convert at roughly 2–3x the rate of text testimonials for this type of offer.

## 13. NO "COST OF INACTION" SECTION

**Severity: Medium**
**Location: Should be on Page 1, between "Why Now" (Section 3) and "Solution" (Section 4)**

Brunson always pairs future pacing (positive: imagine your life with this) with the cost of inaction (negative: imagine your life in 6 months if you don't do this). You have strong future pacing throughout the funnel but the cost of inaction is only hinted at in Section 2 (the problem) and never revisited.

The "Why Now" section (Section 3) gets close with "every month you stay invisible is a month someone else in your space gets stronger" but it's framed as a market observation, not a personal consequence.

**The fix:** Add 2–3 sentences at the end of Section 3 (Why Now) that make it personal:

> "Six months from now, you'll either have a library of content that's working for you around the clock, or you'll be reading another sales page for another challenge, still telling yourself you'll start when you feel ready. Both versions of you exist right now. This is where you choose which one wins."

This isn't fear-mongering. It's an honest observation about the behavioral pattern your audience already knows they have. It works because it's true.

## 14. THE PAGE 2 → PAGE 3 TRANSITION IS ABRUPT

**Severity: Low-Medium**
**Location: Between Page 2 (wizard) and Page 3 (order page)**

When someone clicks "I'M READY FOR VIDEO 2," they go from the wizard (dark, immersive, interactive, personal) to the order page (also dark, but now it's a sales page with paragraphs and an offer stack). The tonal shift is a little jarring.

Page 3 opens with "YOU JUST BUILT YOUR FIRST SCRIPT" which is good — it acknowledges what just happened. But there's no bridge between the emotional high of seeing their script and the rational decision of paying $7.

**The fix:** Add a brief "bridge" element at the very top of Page 3, before the eyebrow. Something visual and emotional, not wordy. A simple element like:

> **Video 1: Built** ✓
> Videos 2–7: Ready to unlock.

This creates visual continuity with the video tracker they just saw on Page 2, reminds them of what they accomplished, and frames the payment as "unlocking" something that's already built and waiting — not buying something new.

## BONUS: THE QUICK WINS (things you can do in under 30 minutes each)

**A. Add a "scripts generated" counter to Page 2.** Even if it starts at a modest number. "2,847 scripts built and counting" next to the wizard title. Social proof without testimonials.

**B. Add one line of micro-copy under the Page 2 "BUILD MY VIDEO 1 SCRIPT" button:** "Takes about 8 seconds. No email required." Reduces friction at the final wizard step.

**C. Bold the $7 price on Page 3 offer total.** It's there but it doesn't visually POP. The contrast between $285+ (crossed out) and $7 should be dramatic. Consider making the $7 significantly larger font and teal-colored.

**D. Add David's photo somewhere on Page 3.** Page 1 has a photo placeholder in the story section. Page 3 has none. A small circular photo near the guarantee or sign-off adds trust at the payment page.

**E. On Page 6 (thank you), add a "Share your script" social element.** After they buy, they're excited. Give them a way to share their Video 1 script (or just the fact that they joined) on social media. This is free traffic generation.

## IMPLEMENTATION PRIORITY

If I had to pick the top 5 to do first:

1. **Fix the "No Upsell" trust breach** (Item 1) — this is the only thing in the funnel that's actively harmful
2. **Add social proof to Page 2** (Item 2) — highest leverage, smallest effort
3. **Write the order bump copy** (Item 5) — direct revenue impact, the seed is already planted
4. **Add "If all this did was..." to Page 3** (Item 3) — proven Brunson close technique
5. **Add urgency via cohort model or momentum framing** (Item 6) — biggest conversion gap

Everything else can be phased in over the next few weeks as you build toward launch.
