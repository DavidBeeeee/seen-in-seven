Day 2... Send at 9am

The Epiphany

Subject line: The thing I got completely wrong about content

Purpose: Deliver the belief shift. Using prepared words is not cheating... they are the missing piece.

Hey [first name],

So I started making videos. And they were bad. Not "charming rough around the edges" bad. Just bad. I would stare at the camera, start talking, trail off into something that made sense in my head but landed nowhere, and then watch the footage back and genuinely wonder why anyone would take business advice from a person who couldn't finish a sentence.

I tried talking points. Bullet lists on a sticky note. Recording myself ten times and picking the least terrible take. Nothing worked, because the problem wasn't that I needed more takes. The problem was that I was showing up to film something I hadn't actually thought all the way through yet.

The thing that changed everything was almost embarrassingly simple. I started writing out exactly what I wanted to say before I said it. Not a teleprompter. Not a word-for-word script I was going to read stiffly at the camera. Just the actual shape of the idea: here's the opening, here's the middle, here's where I'm going with this. And then I'd go film it.

The first video I made that way took one take. One. After weeks of ten-take sessions that still produced garbage, I did one take and it was done.

That's the whole thing. That's the unlock. It isn't confidence and it isn't equipment and it isn't some special version of yourself that shows up once you feel ready. It's just knowing what you're going to say before the camera turns on.

That's what the script builder is built around, and that's what the challenge gives you for all seven videos.
