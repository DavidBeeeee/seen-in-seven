The thing nobody tells you about waiting

The insight you're sitting on has an expiration date. Not because it stops being true. Because someone else is going to say it first.

Right now, somewhere in your space, someone with half your experience and a fraction of your clarity is filming a version of the thing you've been thinking about. Their version is louder. It's less precise. It misses the part that actually matters. But it's going to exist on the internet before yours does if you don't film today.

And here's what that costs you that you can't get back. Once their version is out there, yours doesn't land as a revelation anymore. It lands as a response. An echo. The thing that came second. The audience that needed your specific lens on this will find their version first, and most of them will never know yours existed.

You've spent years developing a way of seeing something that is completely unique to your experience, your failures, your observations. That's not nothing. That's the thing people will share. But only if it gets out of your head and onto a screen.
